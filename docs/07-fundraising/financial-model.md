## 2. Methodology — the bottoms-up chain

Revenue is **never** assumed top-down. Each month it is rebuilt from supply and demand primitives. Monthly for 36 months (Jan-2027 = M1; Y3 = 2029), then Y4–Y5 are projected annually off the Y3 exit.

```
builders (seeded + monthly growth)
  × builder activation                → active builders
  × agents per active builder         → published agents      [SUPPLY]

active buyers (S-curve: ramp to M6 floor, then decaying growth − churn)
  capped by marketplace liquidity (buyers an agent base can serve)   [DEMAND]

active buyers × runs per buyer/mo     → runs
runs × avg GMV per run                → GMV                    [LIQUIDITY → GMV]

GMV × effective take rate             → take-rate revenue
  (effective take = standard take × honeymoon drag, ramping to full over 18 mo)
GMV × infra margin %                  → infra-margin revenue
active builders/buyers × plan ARPU    → subscription revenue
  sum                                 → TOTAL REVENUE          [REVENUE]
```

**Why an S-curve for demand.** Pure monthly compounding explodes unrealistically over 30+ months; a flat growth rate stalls against churn. We use a **decaying-growth S-curve**: high initial monthly growth (~23–25%) that decays toward a steady-state floor (~10–12%), net of monthly churn. This produces the "fast then maturing" shape real marketplaces follow and keeps Y3/Y5 totals on the SOM path.

**Why a honeymoon drag.** `business-plan.md` §5.2 prescribes a **0%-to-traction honeymoon** (Founding Builders pay little/no take early). We model this as an *effective* take rate below the headline standard rate in Y1, ramping to the full rate over 18 months, then stepping to the **mature take rate** (15/18/20%) in Y4–Y5 as the honeymoon fully rolls off.

## 3. Revenue streams (consistent with `business-plan.md` §5.1)

1. **Marketplace take rate** — % of GMV on every paid run. Primary stream. Headline 10–15% (`bizplan`), realized via a blended effective rate that respects the honeymoon and steps to a mature 15–20% (`research` SOM take rate).
2. **Infrastructure margin** — metered compute/inference/tool-call/storage markup, layered *on top* of the take rate (the "Apify hidden margin engine," `apify` §1.3/§1.4). Modeled at 3.5–4.5% of GMV. This is why the headline creator split stays generous while platform economics still earn on every run.
3. **Subscriptions** — builder Pro/Scale plans + buyer platform plans, blended ARPU (Apify Starter/Scale precedent, `apify` §1.2). Small relative to take + infra, as expected for a usage-metered marketplace.

Enterprise / value-added services / agent-payment-rail fees (`bizplan` streams 4–6) are **not yet revenue-bearing in this model** — they are upside (Phase 2/3) and deliberately excluded to keep the model conservative and tied to day-one mechanics.

## 4. Cost structure & P&L logic

**COGS** (drives gross margin ~80–85%):
- Direct infra cost per run (compute/inference/sandbox), ~$0.022/run `[est]`.
- Payment processing ~2.9% of GMV (Stripe-style) `[est]`.
- Payout handling / merchant-of-record tax ~0.4% of GMV (Stripe Connect + MoR, `bizplan` §7) `[est]`.

**Operating expense** (deliberately growth-investment-heavy so the model shows a *credible* burn that justifies the raise, then trends to capital-efficient profitability per the Apify thesis):
- **R&D** (50% of headcount): runtime, marketplace, console, SDK, plus the eval/trust moat.
- **S&M** (32% of headcount + programs): Apollo outbound, product-led SEO, community/Academy, featured placement. Programs scale at ~2.2% of GMV with early floors.
- **G&A** (18% of headcount + non-payroll): trust & safety ops, EU AI Act compliance, finance, legal.
- Loaded cost $165K/FTE/yr. Headcount deliberately lean (Apify ran ~$25M ARR on ~160 FTE; `apify` §4).

**Cash / runway / implied raise.** Annual opex is spread across months on a ramp; monthly EBITDA accrues to cash. The **implied raise** = the 36-month cumulative cash trough (max drawdown from zero) + ~6 months of burn buffer, rounded to a clean number. Opening cash = that raise. See `pnl-and-cash.csv` CASH SUMMARY block per scenario. **This implied raise is the *cumulative* capital required across the full 36 months (seed + a planned Series A) — it is NOT the seed ask.** The seed ask is a lean **~$2.0M ($1.5–2.5M) for ~18 months** (see `use-of-funds.md`); the Series A funds the growth-staffed opex over the back half that drives the ~$5–9M cumulative figure shown here.

## 7. Assumptions to pressure-test (the ones that move the answer)

Ranked by sensitivity of the output to the driver:

1. **Avg GMV per run ($3.20–$3.80) × runs per buyer (78–110/mo).** These two multiply directly into GMV — the single biggest lever. They're the least-anchored numbers (interpolated from pricing benchmarks + agents-per-employee ratios, not a reported run-frequency figure). If real run frequency is half, GMV halves. **Validate against early cohort telemetry ASAP.**
2. **Active-buyer S-curve (initial growth, decay, floor).** Whether the marketplace sustains ~10–12% steady-state monthly active-buyer growth for 30 months is the core "does liquidity compound" bet. Churn (3.5–5%/mo) is the counterweight; SMB long-tail churn could run hotter.
3. **Builder→agent→liquidity supply ramp.** We assume the Founding Builders seed (35–60) compounds at 8.5–11.5%/mo and that supply rarely gates demand. If supply seeding stalls (Apify-pool recruitment underperforms), the liquidity cap binds and GMV falls.
4. **Effective take rate path (honeymoon → mature).** The 0%-to-traction honeymoon depresses Y1–Y2 revenue intentionally; the step to 15–20% mature take in Y4–Y5 is what gets base to ~$54M Y5 take rev. If competitive pressure forces a permanently lower take, revenue compresses ~linearly.
5. **Infra margin % of GMV (3.5–4.5%).** Pure assumption — Apify's real infra gross margin is undisclosed (`apify` §7). Upside if metered inference/tool-call markup is richer.
6. **Opex intensity / EBITDA shape.** We chose growth-heavy opex so EBITDA stays modest and the raise is credible. If you want a leaner "Apify-like" story, headcount and S&M-% can drop and Y3 EBITDA rises sharply (the model already shows base/aggressive profitable in Y3 even at this opex level).
7. **CAC rising with scale.** `unit-economics.csv` shows CAC dipping mid-life then rising in Y4–Y5 as easy demand saturates and paid spend grows — pressure-test whether product-led SEO (Apify's 14× organic engine, `apify` §5.1) keeps blended CAC lower than modeled.

## 8. What this model deliberately excludes (conservatism)

- Enterprise, value-added services, and agent-payment-rail fees (Phase 2/3 upside).
- Working-capital timing on payouts (the 11th/14th payout cycle, `apify` §2.5) — treated as cash-neutral.
- Financing cash flows beyond the single implied primary raise (no debt, no second round modeled in-window; conservative would need one).
- FX, taxes below EBITDA, and depreciation (EBITDA is the bottom line shown).

These keep the model a clean, driver-transparent revenue-and-burn engine that ties to the research and is easy to pressure-test line by line.

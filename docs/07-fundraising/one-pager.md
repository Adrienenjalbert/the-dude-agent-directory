# The Dude — Investor One-Pager

> **The universal marketplace for AI agents — "Apify × App Store for AI agents."**
> Publish any agent, get paid on every run. Hire any agent, trust it works.
>
> **Stage:** Pre-seed / Seed · **June 2026** · **Live MVP:** https://adrienenjalbert.github.io/the-dude-agent-directory/
> *(Headline financials reference `financial-model.md`. "The Dude" is a working placeholder name.)*

---

**The problem — both sides are broken.** Builders can't get distributed or paid fairly (~0.07% of indie devs reach $10K MRR; GPT Store payouts never broadly launched; Apify is retiring rentals in 2026, dropping builder revenue 40–70%). Buyers can't tell what works (**95% of enterprise AI pilots fail**; only ~2–17% of agents reach production vs. 62–72% experimenting). Every existing player fixes at most one side.

**The solution.** A **vendor-neutral, cross-vertical, usage-metered two-sided marketplace** where anyone publishes an agent of any type and earns when others rent/run it. We own the **run + billing + trust layer** — not just the listing — across five surfaces: Discovery/Directory, Publishing/SDK, sandboxed Execution Runtime, Monetization/Payouts, and Trust/Console/Governance. **Bring any framework** (LangChain, CrewAI, AutoGen, custom); MCP/A2A-native.

**Why now (12–24 month window).** Standards matured (MCP ~97M downloads, A2A 100+ orgs, Linux-Foundation-governed); payment rails arrived (AP2, ACP, x402); models commoditized (GPT-4 API −90% → value moved to the distribution/trust layer); ~40% of IT budgets shifting from SaaS seats to agentic platforms. Plus a recruitable supply shock: **Apify's rental sunset** (Oct 2026) creates a pool of monetization-savvy builders ready to move.

**The wedge.** On two axes — openness of publishing × creator payouts on usage — the **open top-right quadrant is empty** for a mainstream, cross-vertical agent marketplace. Payout-paying marketplaces are vertical-locked (Apify=automation, GitHub=dev, Olas=crypto); open ones have broken/absent creator economics (GPT Store, directories). Incumbents are ecosystem-locked and ignore the long tail (Salesforce explicitly excludes small buyers).

**Market.**

| Lens | 2030 | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **TAM** — rentable AI-agent spend | **~$50–55B** | Standalone agent market, 40%+ CAGR from ~$8B (2025) |
| **SAM** — 3rd-party marketplace-distributed | **~$9–11B** | ~18–22% of TAM |
| **SOM** — platform revenue (Yr 3–5) | **~$15–60M base / up to ~$155M** | ~$90M→$330–780M GMV × 15–20% take rate |

Supply is exploding (**>1M agents built/quarter**, +130% QoQ; IDC: 1.3B agents by ~2028); demand is inflecting (72% using/testing, 84% increasing investment).

**Business model.** Take rate on GMV + **metered infrastructure margin** (the Apify engine). Supply magnet: Founding Builders at ~6–10%, a **0%-to-traction honeymoon** stepping to **~10–15%** standard, and **pay creators on ALL paid usage** (closes Apify's free-user gap). Be the most creator-generous credible marketplace; earn via volume + infra margin, not a high headline cut.

**Differentiation & moat.** Trust/evals (verified metrics, automated evals, tiered vetting), creator economics, world-class UX, MCP/A2A interoperability. Compounding moats: liquidity flywheel, product-led SEO content, trust/eval data competitors can't replicate without the runs, and switching costs from analytics + payout history.

**GTM.** Solve cold-start by seeding **supply first** (25–50 Founding Builders incl. a targeted **Apify-migration campaign**), concentrating demand into 2–3 dense categories, then compounding via **product-led SEO** (every agent = an indexable page), **Apollo-powered outbound** (enrich-on-signup, both sides, day one), and **community + open SDK/Academy**. PLG self-serve with sales-assist on PQL signals.

**Traction.** **Live MVP shipped** (directory, agent detail pages, publish/hire flows demoable now) — proof of execution. Backed by a deep, validated research base (market sizing, 20+ competitor teardown, reverse-engineered Apify playbook, GTM + risk register). Next: first Founding Builders signed, private beta in 2–3 categories, first metered GMV.

**Financials (headline, base scenario).** **~$20M total platform revenue by Y3 (~$93M GMV; $14.9M take + infra + subs), EBITDA-positive in Y3 (+$5.8M)**; **~$69M by Y5 (~$301M GMV)**; aggressive up to ~$134M Y5. **Capital-efficiency thesis: Apify reached ~$25M ARR on ~$3M raised** — we raise to *accelerate* a structurally lean take-rate business, not to subsidize one. *(Bottoms-up detail and all scenarios in `financial-model.md`.)*

**The ask.** Raising **~$2.0M seed ($1.5–2.5M band)** for ~18 months to Series A readiness (~$1–3M ARR + proven liquidity). Allocation: **~40% engineering** (runtime + frontend), **~20% GTM** (Apollo/SEO/community), **~15% supply seeding**, **~15% trust/evals**, **~10% G&A/compliance**. **Seed: ~$2.0M / 18 months to Series A readiness; cumulative capital through Series A ≈ $5–9M (see `financial-model.md`, by scenario)** — the seed gets to Series A, the Series A funds the growth-staffed opex behind the higher cumulative figure. *(Full breakdown in `use-of-funds.md`.)*

**Vision.** The neutral run + billing + trust layer for the agent economy — from humans hiring reliable agents today to **agents renting agents** tomorrow (A2A over MCP/A2A + x402/AP2). The preconditions converged, the incumbents are locked out of our segment, and we've already shipped. **The window is 12–24 months.**
